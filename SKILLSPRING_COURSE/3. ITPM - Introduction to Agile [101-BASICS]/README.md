# 1. Agile and Agile Development Cycle

## What is Agile?

Agile is an iterative and incremental software development methodology that focuses on:

* Customer collaboration
* Continuous improvement
* Early delivery of working software
* Responding quickly to changing requirements
* Frequent stakeholder feedback

Instead of completing the entire project at once, Agile delivers the product in small, manageable increments called **iterations or sprints**.

## Agile Development Cycle

1. Requirement Gathering
2. Planning
3. Design
4. Development
5. Testing
6. Deployment
7. Review & Feedback
8. Maintenance

The cycle repeats until the complete product is delivered.

# 2. Agile Methodologies

## Scrum

Scrum is the most widely used Agile framework.

### Key Roles

* Product Owner
* Scrum Master
* Development Team

### Scrum Events

* Sprint Planning
* Daily Scrum (Stand-up)
* Sprint Review
* Sprint Retrospective

### Features

* Time-boxed Sprints
* Product Backlog
* Sprint Backlog
* Increment Delivery

---

## Kanban

Kanban is a visual workflow management method.

### Characteristics

* Uses Kanban Board
* Work is pulled rather than pushed
* Continuous delivery
* Limits Work In Progress (WIP)
* Improves workflow efficiency

---

## Extreme Programming (XP)

XP focuses on producing high-quality software through engineering best practices.

### Practices

* Pair Programming
* Test Driven Development (TDD)
* Continuous Integration
* Refactoring
* Small Releases
* Customer Involvement

# 3. Agile Values, Principles, and Methodologies

## Four Agile Values

1. Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
2. Working software over comprehensive documentation
3. Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
4. Responding to change over following a plan

---

## Twelve Agile Principles (Brief)

* Deliver valuable software frequently.
* Welcome changing requirements.
* Deliver working software regularly.
* Business and developers collaborate daily.
* Build projects around motivated individuals.
* Face-to-face communication is preferred.
* Working software measures progress.
* Maintain sustainable development.
* Focus on technical excellence.
* Keep solutions simple.
* Self-organizing teams produce the best designs.
* Reflect regularly and improve continuously.

# 4. Benefits and Challenges of Hybrid Project Management

Hybrid Project Management combines **Waterfall** and **Agile** approaches.

## Benefits

* Flexibility
* Better risk mitigation
* Improved collaboration
* Increased stakeholder engagement
* Enhanced transparency
* Faster delivery
* Better adaptability
* Suitable for complex projects

## Challenges

* Complex project management
* Difficult team coordination
* Requires experienced leadership
* Communication gaps
* Process integration challenges
* Increased planning effort

---

# 5. Agile vs Hybrid vs Waterfall

| Feature             | Agile            | Hybrid                   | Waterfall           |
| ------------------- | ---------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------- |
| Development         | Iterative        | Mixed Approach           | Sequential          |
| Planning            | Adaptive         | Partly Fixed             | Upfront             |
| Customer Feedback   | Continuous       | Moderate                 | End of Project      |
| Requirement Changes | Easy             | Moderate                 | Difficult           |
| Delivery            | Frequent         | Phase-wise               | Final Delivery      |
| Documentation       | Minimal          | Moderate                 | Extensive           |
| Risk Management     | Early Detection  | Balanced                 | Late Detection      |
| Best For            | Dynamic Projects | Large & Complex Projects | Stable Requirements |

# Important Agile Roles

---

## Scrum Master

* Facilitates Scrum process
* Removes impediments
* Coaches Agile practices
* Supports continuous improvement

---

* Cross-functional
* Builds and tests the product
* Delivers working increments

---

Responsibilities:

* Review product increments
* Provide feedback

# Key Takeaways

* Agile emphasizes iterative development and customer collaboration.
* Scrum, Kanban, and XP are popular Agile methodologies.
* Scrum includes defined roles, events, and artifacts for efficient project execution.
* Agile values flexibility, rapid delivery, and continuous feedback.
* Hybrid Project Management combines Agile flexibility with Waterfall structure.
* Agile enables early risk identification through iterative development.
* Stakeholder feedback plays a vital role in improving product quality.
* Sprint Retrospectives drive continuous process improvement.
* Scrum Masters facilitate the Scrum framework and remove obstacles.
* Agile is best suited for projects with evolving requirements, while Waterfall works well for fixed and predictable projects.
